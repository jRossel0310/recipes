# Herb-Cured Roasted Salmon

**Source:** personal recipe
**Servings:** 2
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes (plus 30–60 minutes curing)
**Cook Time:** 10–14 minutes

## Nutrition (Estimated per serving, based on 4 portions)

* Calories: ~290
* Protein: ~24 g
* Carbohydrates: ~4 g
* Fat: ~19 g

## Ingredients

**For the dry cure:**

* 1 lb salmon fillet (≈454 g)
* 1 tbsp kosher salt (≈15 g)

**For roasting:**

* 1 tbsp olive oil (≈14 g)
* 2 cloves garlic, finely minced or grated (≈6 g)
* 1 tsp lemon zest (≈2 g)
* 1 tsp Dijon mustard (≈5 g), optional but recommended
* 1 tbsp chopped fresh herbs total dill + parsley work best (≈4 g)

**To finish:**

* Fresh lemon, for squeezing
* Flaky salt
* Extra chopped fresh herbs

## Instructions

1. Combine the kosher salt (plus optional lemon zest and dill) and rub onto the flesh side of the salmon only. Refrigerate for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
2. Lightly rinse or wipe off the excess cure and pat the salmon very dry.
3. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
4. Stir together the olive oil, garlic, lemon zest, Dijon mustard, and chopped herbs, then spread evenly over the flesh side of the salmon.
5. Place the salmon skin-side down on a parchment-lined or lightly oiled tray, flesh side up.
6. Roast for 10–14 minutes depending on thickness, about 10 minutes for thinner fillets and 13–14 minutes for thicker ones. The center should still be slightly translucent when pulled from the oven. Target 114 internal.
8. Rest the salmon for 3–5 minutes.
9. Finish with a squeeze of fresh lemon, a pinch of flaky salt, and extra chopped herbs.

## Notes

* The cure is light and won't over-salt the fish at 30–60 minutes; going much longer will start to firm the texture into more of a gravlax.
* Scaling: the cure ratio (1 tbsp salt + 1 tbsp sugar per lb) holds up cleanly for larger fillets. Adjust roasting time by thickness rather than weight.
