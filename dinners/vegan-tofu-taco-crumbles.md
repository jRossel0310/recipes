# Vegan Tofu Taco Crumbles

**Source:** [itdoesnttastelikechicken.com](https://itdoesnttastelikechicken.com/vegan-tofu-taco-crumbles/)
**Servings:** 4 (makes 2 cups)
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 40 minutes

---

## Nutrition (Estimated per serving)

* Calories: 89
* Protein: 7 g
* Carbohydrates: 3 g
* Fat: 5 g

---

## Ingredients

* 1 tbsp light oil, such as canola or vegetable (≈14 g)
* 1 tbsp soy sauce (≈15 g) *(use gluten-free if preferred)*
* 2 tsp chili powder (≈5 g)
* 1 tsp smoked paprika (≈2 g)
* ½ tsp cumin (≈1 g)
* ½ tsp onion powder (≈1 g)
* ½ tsp garlic powder (≈1 g)
* ¼ tsp cayenne *(optional, for heat)* (≈0.5 g)
* 1 block extra-firm tofu, drained — no need to press (350 g)
* ¾ – 1¼ cups salsa (≈180–300 g)

---

## Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper or lightly grease it.
2. In a large bowl, mix together the oil, soy sauce, and all the spices. The mixture will form a brown, paste-like texture.
3. Crumble the block of tofu directly into the bowl using your fingers, breaking it into small, irregular pieces. Toss the crumbles with the seasoning paste until every piece is evenly coated.
4. Spread the seasoned tofu crumbles in an even single layer across the prepared baking sheet. Bake for 30–35 minutes, stirring every 10 minutes or so. Keep a close eye on the crumbles toward the end to avoid burning. You want them nicely browned — smaller pieces will be darker than larger ones, which is perfectly fine and adds texture variety.
5. Transfer the baked crumbles to a skillet or sauté pan and add ¾ cup of salsa. Stir to combine and heat through over medium heat. Add more salsa as needed depending on how saucy you prefer the filling.
6. Serve hot in taco shells or warm flour or corn tortillas with your favourite toppings such as shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, peppers, red cabbage, cilantro, vegan cheese, vegan sour cream, or hot sauce.

---

## Notes

* Do not press the tofu before using — the excess moisture helps the crumbles absorb the salsa at the end, creating a chewy, meaty texture.
* Stir the crumbles regularly while baking to ensure even browning and to prevent burning at the edges.
* Start with ¾ cup salsa and add more to reach your preferred consistency. Watery salsas may require less; thicker salsas may need a splash of water added.
* These crumbles make an excellent burrito filling in addition to tacos.
* **Make ahead:** The baked crumbles (before adding salsa) can be stored separately and combined with salsa just before serving for best texture.
* **Storage:** Store finished crumbles in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 3–4 days. Reheat in a skillet over medium heat with a splash of salsa or water to refresh.
* **Scaling:** Doubles easily — use two baking sheets and rotate them halfway through baking for even results.
