# Fresh Homemade Ravioli with Cheese Filling

**Source:** [The Clever Carrot](https://www.theclevercarrot.com/2022/10/beginners-guide-to-fresh-homemade-ravioli/)
**Servings:** 4
**Prep Time:** 1 hour 30 minutes
**Cook Time:** 5 minutes (+ 1 hour dough rest)

## Nutrition (Estimated per serving)

* Calories: ~420
* Protein: ~18 g
* Carbohydrates: ~48 g
* Fat: ~16 g

## Ingredients

### Pasta Dough

* 300 g Tipo 00 flour (≈2 ⅓ cups) — Molino Grassi or Caputo recommended
* 3 large eggs + 1 large egg yolk (total cracked weight ≈185 g)
* Olive oil, as needed to make up the difference if eggs are small
* Semolina flour, for dusting

### Cheese Filling

* 100 g whole milk ricotta (≈½ cup), drained overnight
* 100 g mascarpone cheese (≈½ cup)
* 30 g Parmesan cheese, grated (≈⅓ cup)
* 10 g fresh basil leaves, chopped (≈¼ cup loosely packed)
* 1 egg
* Salt and pepper, to taste

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Make the Pasta Dough

1. Add the Tipo 00 flour and eggs to a food processor. Run the machine for 15–20 seconds until a dough forms.
2. Remove the dough onto a lightly floured work surface. Cover and let rest for 5 minutes.
3. Knead the dough for 1–2 minutes until it becomes a smooth ball.
4. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and rest for 1 hour. Use this time to make the filling.

### Step 2 — Make the Cheese Filling

5. Combine the drained ricotta, mascarpone, chopped basil, Parmesan cheese, and egg in a bowl. Season generously with salt and pepper and mix well.
6. Taste and adjust seasoning — the filling should be well-seasoned.
7. Cover and chill until ready to use. This step can be done 1–2 days in advance.

### Step 3 — Roll the Pasta Dough

8. Set up your work station on a large, clutter-free surface. Dust with semolina flour.
9. Line 2 sheet pans with parchment paper. Cut 4 extra pieces of parchment to layer between pasta sheets.
10. Cut the rested dough into 4 equal pieces. Flatten one piece into a disc and keep the remaining pieces covered.
11. Roll each piece through a pasta machine, gradually thinning to about 0.30–0.40 mm (#5 or #6 on a KitchenAid roller attachment).

### Step 4 — Assemble and Cut the Ravioli

12. Lay one pasta sheet on the work surface. Dollop rounded teaspoons of filling down the center, spaced 2 finger-widths apart. You can do 1 or 2 rows per sheet.
13. Place a second pasta sheet on top, like a sandwich.
14. Smooth the top sheet with your fingertips, working around each mound of filling to press out air bubbles. Press to seal the edges and sides.
15. Using a fluted ravioli cutter or large knife, cut into 2½–3 inch squares.
16. Transfer ravioli to a parchment-lined sheet pan dusted with semolina flour. Cover with a towel or inverted sheet pan to prevent drying.

### Step 5 — Cook the Ravioli

17. Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a boil.
18. Gently drop the ravioli into the water in batches. Cook for approximately 3–5 minutes, until they float and the pasta is tender.
19. Remove with a slotted spoon and serve immediately with your choice of sauce.

## Notes

* **Drain the ricotta.** Place ricotta in a fine mesh sieve set over a bowl and drain overnight in the fridge. Skipping this step results in watery, grainy filling. A quick method: spread ricotta on stacked paper towels, press firmly to remove moisture.
* **Ravioli yield:** Approximately 16–32 ravioli depending on size and whether you do 1 or 2 rows of filling per sheet.
* **Flour note:** For the eggs and yolk, the total combined cracked weight should be 185 g. Because egg sizes vary, make up the difference with a small amount of olive oil.
* **Make-ahead timeline:** Day 1 — drain ricotta overnight and prepare sauce. Day 2 (AM) — make dough, filling, and assemble. Day 2 (PM) — cook and serve.
* **Room temperature (1 hr):** Arrange on a parchment-lined pan dusted with semolina. Cover tightly with plastic wrap.
* **Refrigerate (up to 4 hrs):** Arrange as above and chill. Do not refrigerate overnight — the pasta will oxidize and the filling will make the ravioli soggy.
* **Freeze (up to 1 month):** Freeze ravioli uncovered on a sheet pan until solid. Transfer to an airtight container or bag.
* **Scraps:** Dry leftover pasta scraps for 2 days, store in an airtight container, and cook later in soup or with ragù.
