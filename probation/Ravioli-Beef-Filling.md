# Classic Italian Beef Ravioli Filling

**Source:** Personal recipe
**Servings:** 4 (enough for ~32 ravioli)
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 10 minutes (+ 30 minutes chill time)

## Nutrition (Estimated per serving)

* Calories: ~320
* Protein: ~28 g
* Carbohydrates: ~2 g
* Fat: ~22 g

## Ingredients

* 1 lb ground beef (≈454 g)
* 1 tbsp olive oil (≈14 g)
* 3–4 cloves garlic, minced (≈10 g)
* ¼ tsp nutmeg, freshly grated if possible (≈0.5 g)
* 100 g whole milk ricotta, drained
* 30 g Parmesan cheese, grated
* 1 egg (≈50 g)
* Salt and pepper, to taste

## Instructions

1. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef, breaking it up into fine crumbles as it browns, about 6–8 minutes.
2. When the beef is almost fully cooked, add the minced garlic and nutmeg. Stir for about 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Drain any excess fat from the pan. Season with salt and pepper.
4. Transfer the beef mixture to a bowl and let it cool completely.
5. Once cooled, finely mince the beef by hand on a cutting board, or pulse in a food processor (about 8–10 pulses) until it reaches a finely textured, spreadable consistency. You don't want a paste, just something cohesive enough to scoop.
6. Combine the minced beef with the drained ricotta, Parmesan, and egg. Mix well. Taste and adjust seasoning — it should be well-seasoned since the pasta itself is unseasoned.
7. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before assembling. The filling must be cold.

## Notes

* **No food processor?** Use a potato masher or the back of a wooden spoon to press the beef into very fine crumbles while it cooks. This eliminates the need for processing afterward. The ricotta and egg will bind everything together in the bowl.
* **Drain the ricotta.** Place ricotta in a fine mesh sieve set over a bowl and drain overnight in the fridge. This prevents watery, soggy filling.
* **Optional depth of flavor:** Deglaze the pan with a splash of red wine after browning the beef and let it cook off before proceeding.
* **Assembly tip:** This filling is slightly denser than a cheese-only filling, so use slightly less per ravioli to keep them easy to seal.
* **Storage:** Filling can be made 1–2 days in advance and stored covered in the refrigerator.
* **Pairs with:** Fresh homemade ravioli dough. See `fresh-homemade-ravioli.md` for the full pasta dough recipe and ravioli assembly instructions.
