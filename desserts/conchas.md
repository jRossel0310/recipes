# Conchas (Mexican Sweet Bread)

**Source:** Personal recipe
**Servings:** 8 conchas
**Prep Time:** 30 minutes (plus 2 hrs 30 min–2 hrs 45 min rising/resting)
**Cook Time:** 18–20 minutes

---

## Nutrition (Estimated per serving)

* Calories: 620
* Protein: 10 g
* Carbohydrates: 85 g
* Fat: 27 g

---

## Ingredients

### Dough

* 4 cups all-purpose flour, sifted (≈480 g)
* ½ cup sugar (≈100 g)
* 1 cinnamon stick
* ¼ tsp salt (≈1.5 g)
* 6 tbsp butter (≈85 g)
* 1 tbsp instant yeast (≈9 g)
* 1 tbsp vanilla extract (≈13 g)
* 3 eggs
* ¾ cup warm milk, about 110°F / 43°C (≈180 mL)
* Butter for shaping, as needed

### Vanilla Topping

* 1½ cups all-purpose flour (≈180 g)
* 1½ cups sugar (≈300 g)
* 1 cup butter or shortening (≈227 g)
* 1 tbsp vanilla extract (≈13 g)

---

## Instructions

1. In a large bowl, combine the sifted flour, sugar, cinnamon stick, and salt. Cut in the butter using your fingertips or a pastry cutter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
2. Add the instant yeast and vanilla extract. Beat the eggs and stir them into the flour mixture.
3. Slowly pour in the warm milk while mixing until a shaggy dough forms. Remove the cinnamon stick.
4. Knead the dough by hand or with a stand mixer for about 10 minutes, until the dough is smooth, soft, and stretchy.
5. Lightly butter a large bowl and your hands. Shape the dough into a ball, place it in the bowl, cover with a clean towel or plastic wrap, and let rise at room temperature for 2 hours or until doubled in size.
6. While the dough rises, make the topping: mix together the flour, sugar, and butter until the mixture resembles coarse sand. Add the vanilla extract and knead into a smooth, pliable paste. Wrap and refrigerate until needed.
7. Butter two baking pans and line with parchment paper. Once risen, divide the dough into 8 equal balls (approximately 100 g each). Place on the prepared pans, cover loosely, and let rest for 30–45 minutes until puffed.
8. Divide the chilled topping into 8 equal portions. Flatten each portion between two sheets of plastic wrap or parchment into a disc large enough to cover the top of a dough ball. Gently press one disc onto the top of each dough ball.
9. Using a sharp knife, score a classic shell (concha) pattern into the topping — curved lines radiating from the center, or a crosshatch grid.
10. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Place a small oven-safe bowl of water on the bottom rack to create steam. Bake the conchas for 18–20 minutes, until the tops are set and the bottoms are lightly golden. Do not overbake or the topping will crack excessively.
11. Remove from the oven and allow to cool on the pan for 10 minutes before serving.

---

## Notes

* The cinnamon stick is used to infuse flavor into the dry mixture — remove it before adding the eggs and milk.
* Warm milk should be around 110°F (43°C) — too hot will kill the yeast; too cool will slow the rise. Test with your wrist; it should feel comfortably warm.
* Kneading is essential — the dough must be smooth and stretchy before its first rise for a good crumb structure.
* Chilling the topping makes it easier to handle and press onto the dough without sticking.
* The bowl of water in the oven keeps the topping from drying out too quickly during baking, preserving its soft, slightly crisp texture.
* **Topping variations:** Add 2 tbsp cocoa powder to the topping mixture for a chocolate concha topping. Natural food coloring can also be kneaded in for traditional pink or other colored toppings.
* **Scoring tip:** Score only the topping, not the dough itself — a gentle hand is all that is needed.
* **Storage:** Store conchas in an airtight container or wrapped individually at room temperature for up to 2 days. Reheat briefly in the microwave for 10–15 seconds to restore softness.
* **Freezing:** Baked conchas freeze well for up to 1 month. Thaw at room temperature and warm in the oven at 300°F (150°C) for 5 minutes.
* **Scaling:** Recipe doubles well. Divide dough by weight for consistent sizing when scaling up.
