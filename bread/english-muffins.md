# English Muffins

**Servings:** 6 medium 3½" muffins
**Prep Time:** 45 minutes (plus ~3 hours rest/ferment)
**Cook Time:** 20 to 30 minutes

## Nutrition (Estimated per serving)

* Calories: ~245
* Protein: ~7 g
* Carbohydrates: ~45 g
* Fat: ~4 g

## Ingredients

### Preferment

* 1 1/2 cups plus 4 teaspoons Unbleached All-Purpose Flour (≈190 g)
* 3/4 teaspoon instant yeast (≈2 g)
* 1/2 cup plus 3 tablespoons lukewarm milk, whole preferred, 90°F (≈152 g)
* 1/4 cup plus 3 tablespoons lukewarm water, 90°F (≈102 g)

### Dough

* 1 cup Unbleached All-Purpose Flour (≈120 g)
* 1 teaspoon table salt (≈6 g)
* 1 teaspoon granulated sugar (≈4 g)
* 1 tablespoon plus 1 teaspoon unsalted butter, melted (≈18 g)
* 2 to 3 tablespoons yellow cornmeal, for dusting (≈20–30 g)

## Instructions

1. Weigh your flour, or measure it by gently spooning it into a cup and sweeping off any excess.
2. **Make the preferment:** In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, yeast, milk, and water until smooth. The mixture will have the consistency of thick pancake batter.
3. Cover the preferment and let rest at room temperature for 1 to 4 hours. After 1 hour it will have grown slightly with small bubbles on top; after 4 hours it will have doubled in volume, with many bubbles and a slightly sour aroma. Longer fermentation = more flavor.
4. **Make the dough:** Add the flour, salt, sugar, and melted butter to the preferment. Mix by hand or with the handle-end of a wooden spoon to form a soft, sticky dough. Using a wet hand and a light touch, work the dough against the side of the bowl until it begins to smooth out and is tacky.
5. Perform a bowl fold: reach a bowl scraper or wet hand between the side of the bowl and the dough, then stretch the bottom of the dough up and over its top. Repeat 6 to 8 times around the bowl, until the dough resists stretching.
6. Flip the dough so the smooth side is up. Cup your hand and apply pressure toward the bottom of the dough, forcing it into the bottom and side of the bowl to create tension. Cover and let rest at warm room temperature for 1 hour.
7. Perform another bowl fold, then flip and round the dough as before. Cover and let rest at warm room temperature for 1 more hour, or until very puffy and marshmallowy.
8. Use a bowl scraper to gently ease the dough onto a work surface dusted with cornmeal. Gently deflate, sprinkle the top lightly with cornmeal, and pat into a circle 1/2" thick.
9. Using a 3 1/2" round cutter, cut 4 rounds, keeping spacing tight to minimize scraps. Gently bring scrap dough together, pinching to adhere, flatten to 1/2" again, and cut 2 more rounds.
10. Lightly dust a piece of parchment with cornmeal and transfer the rounds onto it. Cover and let rest until puffy, 20 to 30 minutes.
11. Preheat a 10" to 12" cast iron or nonstick skillet over medium-low heat until water flicked on the surface sizzles and takes a moment to evaporate. Alternatively, preheat an electric griddle to 350°F.
12. Carefully transfer the muffins to the preheated pan. Cook until the bottoms are lightly browned and the sides look matte, 10 to 15 minutes; muffins will puff to about 1" thick. Flip and cook the second side until browned, another 10 to 15 minutes.
13. Cool slightly on a wire rack. For the best "nooks and crannies," fork-split before toasting and slathering with butter.

## Notes

* For best results, measure ingredients by weight rather than volume.
* The longer the preferment ferments (up to 4 hours), the more tangy and complex the flavor.
* Prefer square muffins with zero scraps? Skip the cutter and divide the dough with a knife or bench scraper.
* Keep heat at medium-low — too hot and the outsides brown before the centers cook through.
* **Storage:** Store at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 3 days, or freeze for longer storage.
