## Instructions

1. Prep everything before you start - stir fry moves fast. Cut the veggies bite-size, broccoli florets on the small side.
2. Start the rice so it's ready when the stir fry is.
3. Whisk all the sauce ingredients together in a bowl.
4. Cut the chicken into 1-inch pieces, season with the garlic powder and pepper, then toss with the cornstarch.
5. Heat 1 tbsp of olive oil in a deep skillet over medium-high.
6. Cook the chicken in two batches: shake off excess cornstarch, cook 4 minutes, flip, and cook another 3-4 minutes until browned and cooked through. Repeat with the remaining oil and chicken.
7. Cook the onion 1 minute, then add the broccoli and bell pepper and cook 3 minutes until tender-crisp. If using frozen veg, add it straight from the freezer and stir-fry 4-6 minutes until the released water cooks off.
8. Return the chicken, whisk the sauce again, and pour it in. Let it bubble under a minute, stirring.
9. Serve over rice.

## Notes

* This version uses less oil and more chicken than the source recipe.
* The sauce as written coats everything; doubling it mostly adds sugar and sodium.
* Frozen stir-fry veg works great; just avoid mixes with sauce packets.
* No mirin? Dry sherry works.
* Keeps 3-4 days in the fridge.
