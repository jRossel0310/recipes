## Instructions

1. Whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar (or citrus juice), honey or brown sugar, garlic powder, ground ginger, black pepper, and red pepper flakes to make the sauce. If using cornstarch, stir it into the 1 tbsp cold water separately to make a slurry.
2. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken strips in a single layer and cook, turning, for 4–6 minutes until browned and cooked through (165°F internal).
3. Pour the sauce over the chicken and toss to coat. Simmer 1–2 minutes until slightly reduced. If using the slurry, add it now and cook about 30 seconds until the sauce turns glossy and thickened.
4. Serve over rice or alongside vegetables.

## Notes

* Fresh garlic and ginger give the brightest flavor, but the powders make this a fast pantry-friendly weeknight meal.
* Add a splash more honey for a sweeter glaze, or more red pepper flakes / sriracha for heat.
