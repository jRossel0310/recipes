## Instructions

1. Boil the cauliflower in generously salted water until completely tender, 10-12 minutes.
2. Meanwhile, season the chicken cutlets with salt and pepper and pat dry, flattening any uneven ones. Sear in a greased skillet over medium-high heat, 2-3 minutes, then flip and cook another 3-4 minutes to 165°F. Rest, then slice.
3. Transfer the cauliflower to a blender with half the milk, both cheeses, the garlic, bouillon, and salt. Blend until smooth, adding the remaining milk as needed to reach an alfredo consistency.
4. Cook the pasta al dente in salted water. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water, drain, and toss the pasta with the sauce in a large skillet, adding pasta water a splash at a time until glossy. Top with the sliced chicken and more Parmigiano.

## Notes

* No blender? An immersion blender or food processor works, just not quite as smooth.
* Keeps 3 days in the fridge; reheat with a splash of milk to loosen the sauce.
