## Instructions

1. Thinly slice the bell peppers and onion into strips roughly ¼-inch thick. Aim for uniform thickness so they cook evenly.
2. Heat the avocado oil in a large cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat for a few minutes until sizzling hot.
3. Add the peppers, onions, chili powder, paprika, cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook for 7–8 minutes, stirring occasionally. For more char, let the veggies sit for a minute or two before stirring.
4. During the last minute of cooking, add the minced garlic to an open area of the pan and cook for 30 seconds, or until fragrant.
5. Mix everything together, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Remove from heat and serve.

## Notes

* **Spice level:** Add thinly sliced jalapeños or serrano peppers for extra heat. Include the membranes and seeds for even more kick.
* **Onion swap:** Red onion gives a pungent bite; sweet or yellow onion will produce a mellower flavor.
* **Getting a good char:** A cast-iron skillet is essential. Stainless steel also works, but non-stick pans will not produce charred edges.
* **Prep ahead:** Slice the vegetables and store them in separate airtight containers in the fridge up to 3 days in advance.
* **Storage:** Refrigerate cooked fajita veggies in an airtight container for up to 5 days, or freeze in a zip-top bag with air removed for up to 4 months.
* **Serving ideas:** Serve on tacos, in burrito bowls, alongside cilantro lime rice, or as a side for any Tex-Mex meal.
